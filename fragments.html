New Molecular Structures with AI

AI based models are offering powerful tools for molecular design and discovery. Here we developed a method to design non-fullerene acceptors (NFA) for organic photovoltaics (OPV) which combines structural analysis and correlation with properties, with a rapid evaluation by machine learning based model, and verification by first principles calculations. Molecules of dataset are first separated into chemical segments, which are evaluated separately. Combinations of high performing segments are selected to form molecules, properties of which are predicted by a machine learning based model, with the highest performing molecules then evaluated by atomistic calculations. We demonstrate this leads to formation of high efficiency molecules.

NFA molecules are conjugated hydrocarbons, with the most efficient structures found to be of the type Donor-Acceptor-Donor. An algorithm was developed that identified fragments including donor group, acceptor group, side chains, and conjugated linkers, based on SMILES strings, and cleaved molecules in a dataset into fragments. The fragments were classified according to relavant optical and electronic properties of the molecule. Highest performing fragments were combined into novel molecular structures, based on fragment tags attached to individual SMILES initially. A graph based neural network was trained to correlate the structures of molecules in the dataset to their properties, and used to predict the properties of novel molecules. The molecules with the most suitable set of properties were skimmed out, and validated by the calculation of their properties by first principle methods. This was found to lead to generation of highly efficient molecules with a systemetic workflow.
