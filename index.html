About me

I explore physical phenomenon in organic devices, to gain insights into charge transport mechanisms in organic materials and at their interfaces, and find ways to enhance device performance by effecient molecular design.

My approach combines fabrication and characterization of test structures to extract information about devices and their components, with first principles simulations to reveal phenomenon at molecular and nano scale. I develop device models, analytical models, numerical models, or AI models to describe and explain these multiscale observations and predict device behaviour based on material properties. I uncover structure-property correlations in organic molecules to identify features that lead to enhanced device characteristics.

At other times, I enjoy sports and have been a part of IIT Bombay tennis team. I have also completed high altitude treks in the Himalays.

Research

Design and Evaluation of Non-Fullerene Acceptors

A computational framework is being developed to design organic molecules for bulk heterojunction solar cells. A multiscale model combining quantum chemical calculations with kinetic monte carlo based algorithm to calculate solar cell parameters from molecular structures of donor and acceptor. A machine learning based framework to design more efficient molecules with optimal properties for high performing soalr cells.

Neural Network Predicting Molecular Properties

We present a graph attention network that predicts accurately ground state and excited state energy of molecules, with only molecular structural features as input.

Origin of Contact Resistance in Organic Devices

Demonstration of how microscopic phenomenon at organic-inorganic interfaces determine the process of charge injection.
