About Me

I am an Early Career Fellow at Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar, India. My research is in the field of electronic devices of organic semiconductors, and charge transport in disordered media. Previously I have also worked on nanoelectronic and MEMS devices; with a focus on modeling device phenomenon and physics of charge transport in materials and at interfaces.

Highlights

Self Assembled Monolayers in Transistors

  • Reduced contact resistance of Cu contacts in pentacene OFETs by functionalizing with SAM to values comparable to Au
  • Established a way to evaluate performance of SAM at contact by measurement of single device and analytical model, rather than measurement of multiple devices with varying channel lengths as required by transfer length measurements (TLM)
  • Deduced contribution of orbital delocalization in SAM in reducing contact resistance based on first principle computations of interface
  • Demonstrated novel chage doping based mechanism of SAM functionalized electrodes, a pradigm shift from work function modulation as mechanism
  • Developed computational model to predict contact resistance from interface properties of SAM functionalised contacts in given OFET

Charge Transport in Polymers

  • Measured temperature dependent characteristics of OFETs of various polymers to deduce mechanism of charge transport
  • Performed first principle computations to obtain monomer properties such as reorganization energy or charge transfer integral which influence charge transport
  • Showed that side chain groups affect transport via reorganization energy, a paradigm shift from the usual understanding
  • Developed a multiscale Monte Carlo based algorithm to simulate temperature dependent charge transport in polymers which decouples effects of thinfilm properties from local coupling, to analyse charge transport parameters accurately.
  • Developing computational model to obtain accurate exciton dissociation rates at polymer hetero-interfaces
